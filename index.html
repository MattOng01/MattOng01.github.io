Kansas City House Price Appreciation
→
Spatial Economics Real Estate Housing Markets GIS

Analyzed five-year cumulative home price appreciation across 198 ZIP codes in the Kansas City MSA using Zillow ZHVI data. Built cross-sectional OLS models with interaction effects to test whether spatial centrality or baseline price tier better explains metropolitan housing returns. Found strong evidence of β-convergence — lower-priced ZIP codes appreciated faster — while proximity to the urban core had negligible explanatory power once starting price tier was accounted for. Preferred model explains approximately 35% of cross-sectional variation in appreciation.

CAPM: Estimating Apple's Market Sensitivity Against the S&P 500
→
Financial Economics CAPM APPLE R

Applied CAPM to estimate Apple's beta against the S&P 500 using daily log returns from 2020 to 2025. Estimated beta of 1.26 indicates Apple moves roughly 26% more than the market. The model explained 57% of Apple's excess returns, with a predicted annual return of 11.12% against a realized return of 11.92%. Discusses applications in portfolio construction and event-study legal analysis.
